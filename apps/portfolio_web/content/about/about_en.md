# ⚐  Why Product → Engineering?

I wanted to own the entire solution.

Not just defining the problem, but taking responsibility for the entire journey — from problem definition to architecture, implementation, and delivery.

- Understand the problem.
- Design the system.
- Build the solution.
- Deliver the outcome.

Engineering gave me the ability to close that loop.


# ⚐  What does my product background bring to Engineering?

10 years of building products as product manager taught me 3 capabilities.

**Define the Right Problem**

    - Why it matters: Without a clearly defined problem, success cannot be measured.
    - Without it: Evaluation becomes subjective, scope continues to grow, and projects become difficult to close.

**Design for Change**

    - Why it matters: Business requirements never stop evolving.
    - Without it: Every new requirement increases delivery time, maintenance cost, and architectural complexity.

**Create Shared Understanding**

    - Why it matters: Different teams have different perspectives and goals. A successful solution requires a shared understanding before implementation begins.
    - Without it: Teams solve different problems, decisions become inconsistent, and the final solution is difficult for everyone to support.


# ⚐  What Have I Built?

I didn't start with a long-term roadmap.
I started by solving actual problems at hand.
Only later did I realize they were part of a larger system.

**Decision Engine**
- **Problem**: Business decisions were difficult to explain, validate, and maintain.
- **Built**: A policy-driven decision engine where every reasoning step is explicit and traceable.
- **Uncovered**: Reliable decisions depend on structured knowledge.

**Question Forge**
- **Problem**: Educational content was locked inside PDF documents.
- **Built**: An AI workflow that converts PDFs into structured Question Objects.
- **Uncovered**: AI-generated code quickly became difficult to maintain. That led me to explore AI Coding Governance.

**CV Base + Compiler**
- **Problem**: Every resume update meant rewriting the same experience again.
- **Built**: A narrative-based knowledge system where resumes and portfolios come from the same source.
- **Uncovered**: Rendering documents without losing hierarchy required a **compiler**.



# ⚐  Where Am I Going?

I'm interested in one question:
   
    `How do people, organizations, and AI systems learn?`

Although they operate in different environments, they all follow the same learning loop.

```text
Knowledge
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Action
      ↓
Feedback
      ↓
Learning
```

My long-term goal is to build systems that make this loop easier to understand, improve, and scale
