The essence of design is not local optimization. It is restoring and reinforcing the feedback loops that enable long-term growth.

Optimization improves metrics within a fixed system. Restoring reconnects a broken loop. Reinforcing strengthens an existing loop.

Many failures are not caused by weak variables but by broken cycles. Improving a node by a few percent has limited impact if the underlying feedback loop no longer functions.

Examples:

City:
Young people leave → Jobs decline → Businesses decline → Tax revenue declines → City attractiveness declines → More young people leave.

Renewal comes from repairing the critical broken link in the cycle rather than optimizing isolated metrics.

Knowledge and product systems:
Document → Question Object → Student Practice → Performance Data → Recommendation → More Practice → Better Data → Question Improvement.

A system begins creating value autonomously when the loop closes and reinforces itself.

Career:
Interview → Offer → Job → Experience → Capability → Better Opportunities.

The first job is not merely optimization; it starts the feedback loop that generates future growth.

Optimization focuses on variables. Restoring and reinforcing focus on loops.

The real design objective is often to optimize the feedback structure of the system rather than the numbers produced by the system.

Many important interventions come from understanding stocks, flows, delays, and feedback loops rather than focusing only on metrics.
