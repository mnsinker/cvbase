The behavior of a system is shaped by the source of its legitimacy and feedback.

Power ultimately answers to the source from which it derives legitimacy.

Different sources of legitimacy produce different incentives, and different incentives lead to different patterns of resource allocation and long-term outcomes.

Examples:

Democratic governments answer to voters.

Public companies answer to shareholders.

Entrepreneurs answer to customers.

Scientists answer to their peers.

AI systems answer to their evaluation and reward functions.

Because systems optimize for what they are evaluated by, the visible behavior of a system often reflects its underlying source of legitimacy rather than the intentions or virtues of the people inside it.

The question is therefore not only:

'What is this system doing?'

but also:

'To whom or to what is this system accountable?'

Understanding the source of legitimacy reveals why resources are directed toward certain goals and why civilizations, organizations, and institutions develop different forms.

Systems optimize for what gives them legitimacy.

More generally:

Every system answers to the source of its feedback.
